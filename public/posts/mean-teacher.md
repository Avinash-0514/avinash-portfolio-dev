# Understanding Mean Teacher for Semi-Supervised Learning

Mean Teacher is a semi-supervised learning method where a student model learns from labeled data and also learns from predictions generated by a teacher model.

In simple terms, the teacher helps guide the student using unlabeled data.

During my research, I explored this method to improve learning when labeled wildfire data was limited.

## Core Idea

The teacher model is not trained directly in the same way as the student. Instead, the teacher weights are updated using an exponential moving average of the student weights.

This helps the teacher become more stable over time.

## Why it matters

- labeled data is expensive
- unlabeled data is often available
- Mean Teacher helps use both

## What I learned

This method taught me how consistency learning works and why stable pseudo-supervision can improve training.
